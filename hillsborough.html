Hillsborough

His longest fight: sixteen years from the Anfield anniversary to the Hillsborough Charter, every step in the public record

← Back to the search

Ninety seven people died as a result of the crush at Hillsborough on 15 April 1989. What follows is Andy Burnham's part in the long road to truth and accountability, told through the official record: Hansard, government publications and parliamentary evidence, each linked to its original source. Type Hillsborough into the search and filter by Hillsborough records to read everything this archive holds.

2009The anniversary that changed everything

As Culture Secretary, Burnham spoke at Anfield on 15 April 2009, the 20th anniversary of the disaster, and was interrupted by the crowd chanting for justice. It is generally treated as the catalysing moment of his campaign. Days later he answered on Hillsborough in the Commons, and the government moved towards full disclosure of the documents, which became the Hillsborough Independent Panel.

Source: his Commons answers on Hillsborough, 20 April 2009 (Hansard via TheyWorkForYou)

2010The Panel survives a change of government

After Labour left office, the new government issued a formal Hillsborough apology and confirmed the Independent Panel would continue its work of disclosing every document held about the disaster.

Sources: the Hillsborough apology, 28 June 2010; Panel to continue, 22 July 2010 (gov.uk)

2012The Panel reports: the truth

The Hillsborough Independent Panel published its report on 12 September 2012: 450,000 pages disclosed, the fans exonerated, the failures and the alteration of statements laid bare. The Prime Minister apologised to the families. The Attorney General moved within weeks to seek fresh inquests, and Burnham spoke in the Commons on the day of the report and in the full debate that October.

Sources: the Panel report, 12 September 2012; fresh inquests application, 16 October 2012 (gov.uk)

2016The inquests: unlawful killing

On 26 April 2016 the new inquest jury determined that the 96 who died were unlawfully killed and that the behaviour of fans played no part. Burnham, then Shadow Home Secretary, gave the Opposition's reply to the Home Secretary's statement the next day, one of the defining speeches of his parliamentary career.

Sources: the Prime Minister's statement, 26 April 2016; the Home Secretary's statement (gov.uk); Burnham's Commons reply, 27 April 2016 (Hansard)

2017The Hillsborough Law

On 29 March 2017, in his final weeks as an MP before becoming Mayor of Greater Manchester, Burnham introduced the Public Authority (Accountability) Bill, the Hillsborough Law: a statutory duty of candour for public bodies, equality of legal funding for bereaved families at inquests, and offences for those who knowingly mislead. The Bill fell with the 2017 election, and securing it in full became the through-line of everything after.

Source: the Hillsborough Law speech, 29 March 2017 (Hansard)

2017The patronising disposition of unaccountable power

Bishop James Jones's review of the families' experiences, published on 1 November 2017, gave the whole story its defining phrase and set out the proposals that became the Hillsborough Charter. Burnham has cited its title ever since as the description of what the families faced.

Source: the Bishop Jones review, 1 November 2017 (gov.uk)

2023Evidence to Parliament, and the Charter

In July 2023 Burnham gave evidence on the Hillsborough Law to the Joint Committee on Human Rights, appearing alongside Bishop James Jones and the families' lawyer Elkan Abrahamson. That December, six years after the review, the government responded: the Hillsborough Charter, six commitments signed on behalf of the government, covering candour, putting public interest above reputation and refusing to defend the indefensible.

Sources: his evidence to the JCHR, 19 July 2023 (Parliament); the Hillsborough Charter, December 2023; the government response (gov.uk)

2025The commitment renewed

In March 2025 the government renewed its commitment to the Charter's principles of openness, transparency and accountability. Burnham has continued to argue for the Hillsborough Law in its full original form, a case he now carries into the leadership of his party.

Source: the Charter commitment, 18 March 2025 (gov.uk)
