About this site

← Back to the search

Inside Andy's Brain is a searchable archive of the public record of Andy Burnham: every House of Commons speech, question and division vote from his years as MP for Leigh (2001 to 2017), his ministerial speeches, his statements as Mayor of Greater Manchester (2017 to 2026), records of his June 2026 return to the Commons as MP for Makerfield, his 2026 Labour leadership campaign, and full-text extracts of the government reports behind his major campaigns.

This site was built by Tom Watson as part of a coding training project. It is an independent, unofficial archive. It is not affiliated with, or endorsed by, Andy Burnham or the Labour Party.

Where the information comes from

Everything here is drawn from publicly available sources, and every search result links back to the original record so you can check it for yourself. Nothing is quoted without a source.

Sources and licences

  • Parliamentary speeches, questions and debates: Hansard, retrieved via TheyWorkForYou. Contains Parliamentary information licensed under the Open Parliament Licence v3.0.
  • Division votes: The Public Whip, an open-source, not-for-profit project. Vote counts are computed from its published data; each record links to the official division page.
  • Ministerial speeches and government publications: gov.uk and the UK Government Web Archive at The National Archives. Contains public sector information licensed under the Open Government Licence v3.0.
  • Mayoral statements: Greater Manchester Combined Authority press releases, 2017 to 19 June 2026, when his mayoralty ended.
  • Press coverage: for copyright reasons, press records here are short summaries with a link to the publisher. No article text is reproduced.
  • Campaign speeches: reproduced from full texts published by the press, with the source named on each record.

What this site is not

It is not a complete account of everything Andy Burnham has ever said. Notable gaps, marked openly in the archive, include broadcast interviews, podcast appearances and Mayor's Question Time transcripts. The search matches words in the records; it does not interpret opinions or positions.

Corrections

Spotted an error? The site and its history are public on GitHub, where corrections can be raised.
